## Anthropic Drops Claude Opus 4.7

The biggest headline of the day. Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.7, and it's currently sitting at #1 on Hacker News with over 220 points and a lively comment thread.

The key upgrades: a 13% lift in coding resolution over Opus 4.6, vision that now handles images up to 2,576 pixels on the long edge (more than 3x previous resolution), and stronger instruction following. That last one is a double-edged sword. The model takes directions more literally, which means existing prompts may need tuning.

There's also a new "xhigh" effort level that slots between high and maximum, giving more control over reasoning depth vs. speed. Pricing stays flat at $5 per million input tokens and $25 per million output tokens. Available through the Claude API, Amazon Bedrock, Google Cloud Vertex AI, and Microsoft Foundry.

**Source:** [Anthropic](https://anthropic.com/research/claude-opus-4-7) | [Hacker News discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/)

## Google Launches Gemini 3.1 Flash TTS

Google released a new text-to-speech model with some genuinely interesting capabilities. Gemini 3.1 Flash TTS supports 70+ languages and introduces "audio tags," natural language commands you can embed directly in text to control vocal style, pacing, and delivery without separate config files.

The model scored an Elo of 1,211 on the Artificial Analysis TTS leaderboard, supports native multi-speaker dialogue, and includes SynthID watermarking to flag AI-generated audio. Google AI Studio offers scene direction, customizable speaker profiles, and exportable parameters.

It also launched on Product Hunt today, alongside a separate launch for Gemini subagents in the Gemini CLI.

**Source:** [Google Blog](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-3-1-flash-tts/) | [Product Hunt](https://www.producthunt.com/)

## OpenAI Updates Its Agents SDK with Sandboxing

OpenAI shipped a meaningful update to its Agents SDK, focused on enterprise safety. The two big additions: sandboxed execution environments where agents only access approved files and tools, and a model-native harness for cross-file workflows.

According to product team member Karan Sharma, the goal is to let companies "build these long-horizon agents using our harness and with whatever infrastructure they have." Python support is live now, TypeScript is coming.

This is a direct response to the growing reality that autonomous agents can be unpredictable when unsupervised. The sandbox approach lets enterprises give agents more autonomy without opening up the entire system.

**Source:** [OpenAI Blog](https://openai.com/index/the-next-evolution-of-the-agents-sdk/) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/15/openai-updates-its-agents-sdk-to-help-enterprises-build-safer-more-capable-agents/)

## Cloudflare Rebrands Browser Rendering as "Browser Run" for AI Agents

Cloudflare renamed its Browser Rendering service to Browser Run and repositioned it as the browser infrastructure layer for AI agents. The update comes with real substance behind the rebrand.

New features include: real-time observability with a "Live View" that lets you watch agent browser sessions as they happen, human-in-the-loop intervention for handling CAPTCHAs and auth, a /crawl endpoint for site-wide content extraction, session recordings as structured JSON, and a 4x increase in concurrency limits (from 30 to 120 concurrent browsers). It also supports MCP clients like Claude Desktop and Cursor.

Available on both free and paid Workers plans.

**Source:** [Cloudflare Blog](https://blog.cloudflare.com/browser-run-for-ai-agents/) | [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/)

## DeepL Goes from Text to Voice Translation

DeepL, the company most people know for its text translator, launched real-time voice translation. It works through add-ons for Zoom and Microsoft Teams (early access), mobile and web conversation tools, and a developer API for custom integrations. There's also a custom vocabulary feature that learns industry terms and proper names.

CEO Jarek Kutylowski was upfront about the challenge: "balancing reducing latency and maintaining accurate results." The initial approach is a three-step pipeline (speech to text, translate, text to speech), with plans to eventually build an end-to-end voice model.

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/16/deepl-known-for-text-translation-now-wants-to-translate-your-voice/)

## Runway's CEO Pitches a 50-for-1 Film Strategy

Cristobal Valenzuela, CEO of AI video startup Runway, suggested that studios could use a single $100 million blockbuster budget to produce 50 films instead, with AI handling the cost compression. His argument: more shots on goal means better odds of a hit.

There's real-world precedent. The AI-assisted film "Bitcoin: Killing Satoshi" reportedly cut estimated production costs from $300 million to $70 million. Amazon, Sony Pictures, and James Cameron have all publicly embraced AI production tools.

The pushback is equally real. Treating filmmaking as a probability game rather than an artistic investment sits uneasily with a lot of people in the industry.

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/16/runway-ceo-says-ai-could-help-hollywood-make-50-films-instead-of-one-100m-blockbuster/)
